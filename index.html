Excerpt from the book

Opening of the first chapter. About where a child’s path in the water really begins.

Doubts are the first thing I hear in almost every conversation with parents. And that is normal. More than that — it is the right reaction.

A parent who has no questions or concerns either has not fully understood what this is really about, or has not truly engaged yet.

Real care always starts with doubt: “Is it safe?”, “What if the child does not like it?”, “Isn’t it too early?”, “Why do we even need this?”

When the topic is water and the sea, these questions become especially sharp.

And this is where one simple thing matters most:
your doubts do not get in the way of your child — they protect them.
